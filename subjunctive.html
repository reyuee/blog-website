The Subjunctive Mood

Grammar · May 12, 2025

The subjunctive is one of those things that confuses a lot of Spanish learners. In English we barely use it but in Spanish it shows up all the time.

What is it?

The subjunctive is a verb mood. It is used when you are talking about things that are uncertain, desired, or emotional not just plain facts.

Remember: It is used for desires, doubts, and unknown situations.

Examples

Here are some common situations where you use the subjunctive:

  • Quiero que vengas — I want you to come
  • Espero que llueva — I hope it rains
  • No creo que sea verdad — I don't think it's true

Notice how the verb changes form after "que". That is the subjunctive in action.

A good trick: if you can put "that" before the second verb in English, you probably need the subjunctive in Spanish.
